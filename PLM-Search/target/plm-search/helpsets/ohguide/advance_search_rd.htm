Quick Search

The Quick Search function is located on the top right corner of your screen under the Step2 - My Query action bar. This function only searches for the search criteria you typed or selected in a particular data field.

 The searches for the Quick Search and main Search page functions are different. When you enter or select information into a Quick Search data field or dropdown list, the LEADS 2.0 application only searches for information in that particular data field or fields.

Example

Type the nickname ‘bob’ (not case sensitive) into the Nickname data field and the Quick Search function only searches the Nickname data field.

 

Example

Type the last name smith into the Last Name data field and the Quick Search function only searches the Last Name data field.

 

If you click the Search button or press the <Enter> key, without making a selection, all parolees in the LEADS 2.0application database will be listed.

 

The Help icon is displayed in the top right hand corner of the Quick Search dialog drop-down box.

Click the Help icon so you can view Help documentation related to the Quick Search function or the entire LEADS 2.0 application.

Click the Quick Search function and the dialog drop-down box displays the Help icon, data search fields, data selection drop-down boxes, the Registration and Felony Notification section, and the Search and Cancel action buttons.

When your results are displayed, the blue Refinements: action bar changed into the Quick Search action bar. The section of the action bar that displays the words Quick Search is now orange showing you where you created your search criteria. Your search criteria information continues to be listed in blue.

Click the Search button or press the <Enter> key to activate your search. The Quick Search dialog box closes and the results of your search are displayed on the search results section of the Search page.

The following Quick Search data search fields related to parolees are displayed in the dialog drop-down box:

o   Last Name

o   First Name

o   Middle Name

o   Alias Last Name

o   Alias First Name

o   Moniker (a parolee’s name, nickname or alias)

o   City

o   Zip Code

o   CDC#

o   Birth Date (mmddyyyy)

o   Parole Release Date Range

o   SSA# (Social Security Administration #)

o   CII# (Criminal Information Index)

o   FBI #

o   License Plate #

o   Vehicle Year – 2 data fields (requires a beginning and ending year) with this format (yyyy)

Type information into data fields to narrow your search criteria. If you want to be very specific with your search, enter data in as many fields as you can. The LEADS 2.0search function will check all fields and returns only parolees who match your criteria.

Example

Type smith (not case sensitive) into the Last Name field, john into the First Name field, smitty into the Alias Last Name field, and click the Search button. Look at the total number of matching parolees. However, when you add 04011992 into the Birth Date field, the total number of matching parolees can decrease by hundreds, possibly several thousand parolees.

When entering dates such as the Birth Date use this format (mmddyyyy). If you don’t enter the requested date format the following error message displays: Error: “Please enter Birth Date (mmddyyyy)”.

Two Parole Release Date fields are displayed. Enter the beginning release date (mmddyyyy) into the first data field and the ending release date (mmddyyyy) into the second data field when you want to search a range of parole release dates.

Two Vehicle Year fields are displayed. Enter the beginning vehicle year (yyyy) into the first data field and the ending vehicle year (yyyy) into the second data field when you want to search a range of vehicle years.

The following selection drop-down boxes are listed on the Quick Search dialog drop-down box:

•            Res(idence) County (##)

•            Birth (County) (##)

•            Ethnicity (##)

•            Hair Color (##)

The Res County and Birth County fields let you click and select your search criteria from a list of counties in the selection drop-down box.

Two Ethnicity selection drop-down boxes are displayed and let you search for more than one ethnicity. Select your first ethnicity choice from the first selection drop-down box. You can pick a second ethnicity to narrow your search. The ‘or’ logic will search for both ethnicities. Parolees matching your first or second choice will be displayed.

Two Hair Color selection drop-down boxes are displayed and let you search for more than one hair color. Select your first hair color choice from the first selection drop-down box. You can pick a second hair color to narrow your search. The ‘or’ logic will search for both hair colors. Parolees matching your first or second choice will be displayed.

Res County, Birth County, Ethnicity, and Hair Color lists are dynamically created and are data driven. They change when data is modified, entered or deleted from the database.

 

Example

The Ethnicity for Native American or the Hair Color orange may not be displayed on the list because none of the parolees entered into the database were identified as Native American or have orange hair.

The Registration and Felony Notification section in the Quick Search dialog drop-down box displays:

Four Check boxes:

•            PC290 Sex Off(ender)

•            PC457.1 Arson

•            HS 11590 Drugs

•            PC3058.6 Violent Felon

Click each check box you want to select as part of your search criteria.

The following selection drop-down boxes:

•            2 SMT (Scars, Marks and Tattoos) Type/Location boxes

•            2 Commitment Offense boxes

Two SMT (Scars, Marks and Tattoos) Type/Location selection drop-down boxes are displayed and let you search for more than one SMT type/location. Select your first SMT choice from the first selection drop-down box. You can pick a second SMT choice to narrow your search.

Click the ‘AND’ Logic if you need LEADS 2.0to search and display only parolees who display both of your SMT choices.

Click the ‘OR’ logic if you want to display parolees matching either of your SMT type choices.

Example

 

‘AND’ logic:

Your first SMT choice was a Humming bird and your second SMT choice was the word Mother. All parolees listed in the results have a Humming Bird and the Mother tattoo.

 

Example

 

‘OR’ logic:

Your SMT choices are the same as above. For this logic, all parolees listed in the results have either tattoos or only one of them.

2 sets of Picture of Tattoo and Text of Tattoo data fields are displayed as part of the Quick Search function. Type your picture and text choice in the both sets of Picture/Text Tatto boxes.

Click the ‘AND’ Logic if you need LEADS 2.0to search and display only parolees who display both of your Picture/Text Tattoo choices.

Click the ‘OR’ logic if you want to display parolees matching either of your Picture/Text Tattoo choices.

Example

 

‘AND’ logic:

Your first Picture/Text Tattoo choice was a Humming bird with the name Jane and your second Picture/Text Tattoo choice was a rose with the word Mother. All parolees listed in the results have a Humming Bird with the name Jane and a rose with the word Mother tattooed on their body.

 

Example

 

‘OR’ logic:

Your Picture/Text Tattoo choices are the same as above. For this logic, all parolees listed in the results have either tattoos or only one of them.

 

NOTE: as noted above, when selecting SMTs, Picture of Tattoo and Text of Tattoo, there are two drop down boxes for each. These can be used individually or as an enhanced search using either an “And” or an “OR” condition. For instance if you select an SMT and use the “And” condition then select a different SMT from the second drop down box, parolees that have both SMTs will be returned. By selecting “OR” parolees with either SMT will be returned. It should also be noted that Picture of Tattoo and Text of Tattoo both use text for searching.

Two Commitment Offense selection drop-down boxes are displayed and let you select more than one offense. Select your offense choices from both selection drop-down boxes.

Click the ‘AND’ Logic if you need LEADS 2.0to search and display only parolees who committed both offense choices.

Click the ‘OR’ logic if you want to display parolees matching either of your offense choices.

Example

 

‘AND’ logic:

Your first offense choice was A1234 and your second choice was B5678. All parolees listed in the results have committed both offenses.

 

Example

‘OR’ logic

Your offense choices are the same as above. For this logic, all parolees listed in the results have committed either offense or only one of them.
