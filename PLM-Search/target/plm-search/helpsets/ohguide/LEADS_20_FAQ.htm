General FAQs


Where does the data in LEADS come from?

LEADS is populated on a near-real time bases from the CalParole Tracking System. CalParole data is compiled from numerous sources with varying reliability to support Parole Agent procedures / in-house use. Data should be verified prior to taking action as it may not be current and critical data may be missing.

CalParole is populated from the following sources: Parole Agents, Criminal History, BPH, Parolee LEAs, CLETS, Criminal History, and Collateral.

How often is data updated in LEADS 2.0?

LEADS 2.0 performs a nightly data refresh, with updates provided every 15 minutes (for near-real time access)

What are the enrollment requirements to become a LEADS 2.0 user?

         Agency must be a Criminal History user of the California Law Enforcement Telecommunications System (CLETS) and in good standing / free of sanctions from DOJ or CDCR

         Required documents signed and submitted by agency official (Agency Participation & Hold Harmless Agreements)

         No cost or financial obligation required to enroll or for on-going participation

         Completed End User Agreement submitted to CDCR, for each individual user


How do I verify the data I find in LEADS?

         Contact the Parole Agent for additional parolee information

  Agent name, phone number and email listed on every Parolee Detail Record

  A message can be sent to the Parole Agents email by clicking on the Agent e-mail link on the Parolee Detail Record

         Contact Warrant Unit for confirmation / additional information at (916) 324-2891 (available 24-hours a day)

         To assist in updating parolee information:

  Contact the Parole Agent (name, phone number listed on every Parolee Detail Record)

  Agent can be contacted directly from Parolee Detail Record by clicking on the Agent e-mail link


Can I get a data download of the whole state?

Yes, you can download parolee data across the state by building a client to access the web service. The download feature in LEADS 2.0 is still limited to geographic grouping.

 

LEADS 2.0 Implementation FAQs:

 

Will my existing LEADS user ID and password allow me to access LEADS 2.0?

Yes, the existing LEADS user IDs and passwords will be moved into LEADS 2.0.

If my password is not compliant, will I be required to change it before accessing LEADS 2.0?

No but when a password is reset in LEADS 2.0, it will require a more complex password with the following characteristics:

         At least eight characters long

         At least one uppercase letter

         At least one lowercase letter

         At least one number


What are the system requirements to use LEADS 2.0?

         Internet browser which applies the Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) & Java scripting

         128-bit encryption US/Canadian version key

         To view photos, a PDF viewer (such as Adobe Acrobat) is required.

         One of the following browsers:

  Internet Explorer 7 or 8

  Mozilla Firefox 3.5 or later

 

In order to utilize the mobile LEADS 2.0 application, does a Personal Digital Assistant (PDA) have to be agency-issued or can an officer use their own personal PDA?

All equipment used to access LEADS must be agency issued. Access to the mobile application is separate from the rest of LEADS 2.0 and must be requested from the LEADS helpdesk. The URL for the mobile application is different from the LEADS 2.0 desktop/laptop application.

Is there a specific approval required to access the data download?

Yes, in order to access the download, the user must have the download function included in their profile. Download users are most commonly crime analysts. Its important to note that no additional support is provided for download users

 

Is there any data available in the download thats not available in LEADS 2.0?

No, LEADS 2.0 contains all the parolee data available on the download

What training materials are available?

On the existing LEADS site, there are three single page overviews available for all users addressing the following:

         What is Different in LEADS 2.0

         How to Search in LEADS 2.0

         NRP Information (which includes Save Search)


* Corresponding online training videos will be available in the near future

Updated Policy and Procedure forms will be available online including

         Parole LEADS 2.0 Agency Participation Agreement

         Parole LEADS 2.0 Hold Harmless Agreement

         Parole LEADS 2.0 End User Agreement

 

         LEADS 2.0 Roll out steps (v2)

 

At a future date there will be materials available for Distributed Account Management, once this functionality is implemented.

NOTE: Training material will be available on the LEADS 2.0/Learning Resources Tab in the near future.

 

LEADS Functional FAQ

Can I search on a partial license number?

Yes, using the wildcard (*) function in the Quick Search or Keyword Search will allow you to search on partial license plate numbers.

How do I start a new search?

Click on the (orange) Search Button/Tab, at the top of the screen, to clear and start a new search. You can also remove restrictive factors (Refinements) in the Parolee Search Results by clicking the x on the blue box displaying the attribute.

Can I save my lineup?

For Photo Lineups, LEADS uses the most current picture of parolees while theyre on parole, and for one year after theyve been discharge. This does not guarantee that the photos will be the same as originally used. By choosing to print the lineup, the lineup is exported to a PDF for printing. This PDF can be saved as a record of the lineup.

How do I search by offense code?

There are three main search Tabs grouped by Personal Information, Parole Data and Vehicle Information. Offense Code information is found under the Parole Data tab.

How will I search for NRP?

NRPs will be grouped under Parole Units starting with NRP or MNRP followed by the region 1-4. On the Parole Data tab, a drop-down list of all Parole Units is available to choose from. For more information on NRP, we have provided an NRP specific overview in the LMS.

What fields are included in the Keyword Search?

The Keyword Search will return the search criteria when found in any of the following fields:

         Drivers license number

         Vehicle license plate

         Special condition information

         Classification description

         Commitment offense code

         Alias Last name

         Alias First name

         Tattoo picture text

         SMT description

         Message phone

         Parole unit

         CII number

         FBI number

         SSA number

         Comments

         Vehicle style

         Vehicle make

         Vehicle model

         Vehicle class

         Full phone

         CDC number

         Last name

         First name

         Middle name

         Moniker

         Care of

         Street

         City

         County

 

What is a Wildcard search?

A Wildcard search is a search that substitutes the asterisk character (*) for all possible characters in a field. The Wildcard will automatically be added at both ends of:

         Tattoo Description

         Tattoo Text

For example: If rose is entered into either of these fields it will be searched as *rose*

The Keyword Search field allows for Wildcard Search.

Is there a limit to the number of searches I can save?

There is no physical limitation on the number of searches you can save, however, a large number of saved searches will slow down the initial load of the search page for you

What is the default sort order for results that are returned from a query?

There is no default sort order but each field in the results can be sorted in ascending or descending order.

 

LEADS Data Questions


What is the CDC#?

The CDC# is a unique 5-digit number {prefaced by one (1) or two (2) alpha character} assigned to each individual parolee:

  • I Interstate Parolee who is paroled from another state and under supervision by CDCR
  • R Interstate Parolee awaiting approval for CDCR supervision (this temporary number will change to an I number if accepted)
  • N0 to N1 Female, Non-Felon, Civil Narcotic Addict
  • N2 to N9 Male, Non-Felon, Civil Narcotic Addict
  • W & X Female, Felon Parolee
  • All others Male Felon Parolees


For parolees with two alpha characters, the first alpha character represents the above.

What do the Registration and Notice codes in LEADS 2.0 mean?

  • HS11590 parolee is required to register as a Narcotics Offender
  • PC 290 parolee is required to register as a Sex Offender
  • PC 457.1 parolee is required to register as an Arson Offender
  • PC 3058.6 CDCR is required to notify law enforcement of the release of a Violent Offender. This indicates parolee has been committed for a crime listed in 667.5(c) PC (which defines a violent offense).


What are the PC290 Notifications in LEADS 2.0?

  • Parolees can be found on the PC290 Registration search when

  PC290 Registrants are within 60 days of release from prison

  PC290 parolees address changes

  PC290 parolees classification changes to HRSO

      Parolees can be found on the PC 290 Discharge search when:

  PC290 Registrants are within 60 days of release from parole

  Contact will be made with LEA for placement of new GPS device

  PC290 parolees are displayed for 15 days on Search Results page


A search audit is recorded every time PC290 data is accessed

How many special conditions are displayed?

CDCR policy requires the Parole Agent to enter the first three special conditions. There is no system limitation to presenting additional conditions if they are entered.
