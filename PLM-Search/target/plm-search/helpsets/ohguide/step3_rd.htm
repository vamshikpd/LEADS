Step 3 – Parolee Results

You can view detailed information for a listed parolee when you click any data field located on the parolee’s data line.

However, you can also browse through more parolee lists when you click one of the underlined numbers, the underlined word Next or the two underlined greater >> than signs. These underlined options are links to groups of data matching your search criteria. They are located to the right of the Refinements: bar.

Example

1 2 3 … Next >> are listed on the right side to let you know that more parolees match your search criteria

Click the word Next and the next sequence of numbers, along with two less << than signs, the underlined and abbreviated word Prev, (Previous), the word Next, and the two greater >> than signs.

Example

<< Prev 11 12 13 … Next >>

Click the << signs to display the previous group of 10 parolees.

The word Next and 2 greater >> than signs won’t be available when you’ve reached the end of matched parolees.

 << Prev 11 12 13

Click the word Prev (Previous) to display the previous 10 group(s) of parolees. Each group includes 10 parolees unless the last group holds less than 10 parolees.

Your keyword is no longer visible in the Keyword Search field.

Move the scroll wheel on your PC mouse forward or backward or click, hold and drag the scroll bar on the right side of your screen up and down to view the list of parolees on your current screen page.

The size of your screen or monitor determines the amount of information you can view on a screen page.

 

The data field columns can be sorted when you click the up and down arrows under the data field name. The matching parolees are listed based on relevance ranking. Each screen page displays data fields for each parolee data line. The data fields include:

•            CDC # (California Department of Corrections number)

•            Last then First Name

•            Birth Date

•            Gender

•            Ethnicity

•            Height

•            Weight

•            Hair color

•            Eye color

•            Unit Name (Parole Unit Name)

•            Status

•            GEO (Geographic Longitude and Latitude)

Unit Name - The Unit Name data field identifies the Parole Unit/Sub-Unit Office where a parolee must report.

There are 193 Parole Unit/Sub-Unit Offices in 84 different locations within the 4 California Regions.

The Status data field identifies a parolee’s status within the Parole System. The status codes were in LEADS2 when the documentation was prepared:

•            Active

•            Discharged

•            Pending

•            Pre-parole

•            Revocation

•            Suspended

•            Suspended NO WNT (With a New Term)

 

The lists in drop-down boxes are dynamically created and are data driven. They change when data is modified, entered or deleted from the database.

  GEO - The Geo field identifies Geographic Longitude and Latitude location where a parolee lives. The world globe displays a Google map with location icons that show the exact location/address of a parolee.           

Click the world globe. You can zoom in (drill down) to street level when you click the red location markers.

Sorting Data

 

You can sort every parolee data column listed on the main Search page below the Step 3 – Parolee Results action bar, one line per parolee. Each column has a data field name with a black up and down arrow. By default, data columns are not sorted. Both arrows are black. A data column is sorted when one arrow is orange.

Click the up or down arrow on a data field name to change the sort order of the data column.

The down arrow sorts the data column in ascending order (A – Z). When the down arrow is orange the data column is sorted from A to Z.

The up arrow sorts the data column in descending order (Z – A). When the down arrow is orange the data column is sorted from Z to A.
