Keyword Search

The Keyword Search is located directly under the Step 2 - My Query action bar.

The Keyword Search field is a free form search mechanism that provides the ability to search almost all parolee detail information at the same time.  You can enter as many keywords or numbers you need for your search criteria. Use search words that you know will be unique and can easily identify a parolee for whom you need more information. Use words like the CDC #, Parolee’s last name or full name, Alias, Street name, City, etc…

Type your keyword(s) (not case sensitive) into the Keyword Search field

Example

sacramento, sacramento street, john smith smitty, V231433 smith, pink, blue

Click the Search button to the left of the Keyword Search field or press the <Enter> key to activate your search. The total number of matching parolees will display on the Step 3 – Parolee Results action bar.

Example

1

Type sacramento into the Keyword Search field and the search function searches all data elements in the LEADS2 database for that one word. More than 8,000 parolees can be found.

Example

2

Type sacramento street into the keyword field and the Search function searches all data elements in the LEADS2 database for both words. This search can find more than 1,000 parolees.

Auto-Corrections

The auto-corrections function is activated when you don’t know the spelling of a keyword or you misspelled your keyword(s).

When you type your search criteria into the Keyword Search field try to be as accurate with the spelling of the word as you possible can. Otherwise, your results may not be what you expect.

You entered misspelled keyword(s):

1.      The auto-corrections function is activated.

2.      It checks the dictionary and tries to find word(s) similar word(s).

3.      The auto-corrections function finds a close match.

4.      LEADS2 performs a search based on the close match.

5.      Parolees are listed.

6.      A message is displayed in the blue section of the Refinements: action bar that includes your ‘misspelled word(s)’, the words ‘autocorrected to’ and the ‘word(s)’ the auto-corrections function found in the dictionary.

Below are 3 examples of misspelled keywords and their auto-correction results:

Example

Enter ‘pink’ into the keyword search field.

The search function finds 91 parolees who have the word ‘pink’ somewhere in their record; maybe in the last name, an alias, or on a tattoo.

Enter ‘pnik’ into the keyword search field.

            ‘pnik’                 ‘autocorrected to’

Searched for ‘pink’. The auto-corrections search function finds the same 91 parolees.

 

Example

Enter ‘pnk blue’ into the keyword search field.

The search function finds 1 parolee who has the words ‘ink blu’ somewhere in their record; maybe in the last name, an alias, or on a tattoo.

 

Example

Enter ‘andersn’ into the keyword search field.

The search function finds 1,700 parolees who have the word ‘anderson’ somewhere in their record; maybe in the last name, an alias, or on a tattoo.

LEADS2 did not display any parolees with the word ‘andersen’ in their records. Use ‘andersen’ and over 30 parolees are listed.

 

The examples let you see that it’s critical to type correct keyword(s)
